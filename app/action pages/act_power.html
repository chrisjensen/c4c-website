Why Switch Power?

Some big corporations are working against renewable energy, spending big money lobbying our government against action on climate change to protect their profits.

These are the same companies that many of us buy our energy from, by telling them you won’t stand for it and switching your power to a better company, you can support energy companies that are helping to make climate action possible and show the dirty energy companies that their actions don’t pay.

In this action, we’ll help you to understand who’s responsible, and what you can do about it.

By taking this action you can also support Climate for Change.

Powershop sponsors Climate for Change every time someone changes to them through our website. So if you switch to Powershop, you’ll be helping to support the work of Climate for Change.

You don’t have to choose Powershop, by choosing any of the top three, you’ll be helping to support renewable energy and the renewable energy target.

If you’re planning to switch to Powershop, please use the link below as it will help us and powershop to know that you switched through us.

3. Will you pay more?

Exit Fees

Some power companies may charge you a fee to switch away from them. You should check the website or call your current energy company to check any fees you may get charged if you change to a different energy company.

You might not have to pay an exit fee

If you’re switching to Powershop, they’ll cover your exit fee (up to $75).

If you’re planning to switch to another energy provider, you could give them a call and ask if they’ll cover your exit fee.

5. Tell your old power company why you’ve switched

For your action to have the most impact, your energy company needs to know why they’ve lost your business so they know their actions on climate change are important to customers.

Send them a quick message to let them know why you've switched.

Tips for writing a message:

  • Tell them you know they lobbied against the RET

  • Include your customer reference number / account number

  • Tell them you’ve taken your business elsewhere (they might not make the connection if you don’t).

  • Be polite. The person reading your message may not even be aware of their employer’s actions.

Click here to see an example

Dear Sir/Madam,

I’m a former customer and I am writing to let you know that I have changed to another power company because of your actions on the RET.

I am concerned about Climate Change and I believe that having an ambitious Renewable Energy Target is important for a safe climate and the future of Australia. I was disappointed to learn that you have lobbied the Government to have the RET scrapped or reduced in 2015.

I hope in future you will chose more responsible climate action.

Sincerely,
